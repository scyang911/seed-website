Battery Technology

🔋 Battery
Solid-State Electrolyte 2025
Halide Solid Electrolytes with Enhanced Ionic Conductivity for All-Solid-State Lithium Batteries

B.-J. Hwang, W.-N. Su, et al. · Advanced Energy Materials

Synthesized a series of Li₃InCl₆-based halide electrolytes via mechanochemical routes. Achieved ionic conductivity exceeding 2 mS cm⁻¹ at 25 °C through controlled stoichiometry. Demonstrated compatibility with Ni-rich cathodes in coin-cell configurations.

Anode-Free 2025
Anode-Free Lithium Metal Batteries: Challenges and Progress in Interface Engineering

M.-C. Tsai, B.-J. Hwang et al. · Chemical Society Reviews

Comprehensive review of anode-free lithium metal battery architectures. Identified electrolyte decomposition at the Cu current collector as the primary capacity-fade mechanism. Proposed SEI pre-formation strategies that extend cycle life beyond 200 cycles.

Sodium Battery 2024
Sulfide-Based Solid Electrolytes for Sodium Metal Batteries with Superior Electrochemical Stability

W.-N. Su, J.-K. Chang et al. · ACS Energy Letters

Developed Na₃PS₄-based sulfide electrolytes with wide electrochemical stability windows (0–5 V vs. Na⁺/Na). Operando X-ray diffraction revealed suppressed sodium dendrite growth. Full cells with hard-carbon anodes delivered 85% capacity retention over 300 cycles.

Cathode Materials 2024
Surface Coating Engineering of Ni-Rich NMC Cathodes for High-Voltage Solid-State Batteries

Y.-S. Ye, F.-M. Wang et al. · Journal of Materials Chemistry A

Applied LiNbO₃ nanoscale coatings on NMC811 cathode particles via atomic layer deposition. Suppressed Ni dissolution and reduced interfacial resistance by 65%. Solid-state full cells operated stably at 4.4 V upper cutoff for 150 cycles.

Membrane Engineering

🧪 Membrane
Osmotic Energy 2025
MOF/COF Hybrid Ionic Diode Membranes for High-Power Osmotic Energy Generation

W.-S. Hung, L.-H. Yeh et al. · Nature Energy

Constructed asymmetric membranes by integrating MOF and COF layers to create ionic diode behavior. Achieved power density of 25 W m⁻² in artificial seawater–freshwater salinity gradients. Ion selectivity exceeded 1000 across a wide pH range.

Desalination 2025
MXene-Based Membranes with Sub-Nanometre Channels for Efficient Ion-Selective Desalination

C.-C. Hu, W.-S. Hung et al. · ACS Nano

Fabricated Ti₃C₂Tₓ MXene membranes with controlled interlayer spacing of 0.35 nm. Demonstrated 99.2% NaCl rejection while maintaining water permeance of 18 L m⁻² h⁻¹ bar⁻¹. Stable performance maintained over 720 hours of continuous filtration.

Ferroelectric 2024
Piezoelectric β-PVDF Membranes with Switchable Ion Transport for Blue Energy Harvesting

L.-H. Yeh, C.-C. Hu et al. · Angewandte Chemie

Electrospun β-phase PVDF membranes exhibited reversible ion transport switching under mechanical pressure. Ferroelectric domain alignment enhanced cation selectivity by 40-fold. Integrated piezoelectric membranes harvested 8 W m⁻² from simulated wave energy.

Hydrogen Energy

⚡ Hydrogen
Water Splitting 2025
Single-Atom Alloy Catalysts for Highly Efficient and Durable Alkaline Water Electrolysis

M.-H. Yeh, H.-C. Huang et al. · Joule

Synthesized Ru single atoms anchored on Ni-Fe layered double hydroxide for OER. Achieved overpotential of 198 mV at 10 mA cm⁻² in 1 M KOH with turnover frequency of 2.3 s⁻¹. Electrolytic cell operated at 1.72 V at 500 mA cm⁻² for 500 hours without degradation.

CO₂ Reduction 2025
Bimetallic CuZn Electrocatalysts for Selective CO₂ Reduction to Ethylene at Industrial Current Densities

D.-Y. Wang, W.-H. Huang et al. · Energy & Environmental Science

Engineered CuZn alloy nanoparticles with optimized d-band center for selective CO₂-to-C₂H₄ conversion. Faradaic efficiency of 72% at −1.1 V vs. RHE with partial current density exceeding 300 mA cm⁻². DFT calculations revealed Zn–Cu synergism stabilizing the *CO intermediate.

Fuel Cell 2024
Ammonia Electrosynthesis via Nitrogen Reduction Reaction on Fe–Mo Dual-Site Catalysts

H.-K. Tian, M.-H. Yeh et al. · Advanced Materials

Developed Fe–Mo bimetallic catalysts supported on N-doped carbon for ambient-pressure N₂ electroreduction. NH₃ yield rate of 68.2 μg h⁻¹ mgcat⁻¹ with Faradaic efficiency of 34.5% at −0.3 V vs. RHE. In situ Raman spectroscopy confirmed *N₂H as the key reaction intermediate.
