International Collaboration

Global Research Network

SEED Center maintains active partnerships with leading research institutions across Europe, North America, and Asia. Our international collaborations span joint publications, researcher exchange programs, co-supervised doctoral students, and industry-focused technology transfer.

Through the SEED Seminar Series, we regularly host distinguished international guests for lectures and research discussions, fostering cross-disciplinary dialogue and long-term partnerships.

Partner Institutions

🇩🇪 Germany
Forschungszentrum Jülich

Helmholtz Research Center · Institute of Energy and Climate Research

Joint research on solid oxide electrolysis cells (SOEC) and in-situ electrochemical gas analysis. Researcher exchange and co-publication program active since 2022.

Hydrogen SOEC
🇩🇪 Germany
Bavarian Center for Battery Technology (BayBatt)

University of Bayreuth · Battery Research Center

Collaboration on solid-state electrolyte characterization and interface engineering. Visiting researcher program with PhD co-supervision on halide and sulfide electrolytes.

Battery Solid-State
🇨🇦 Canada
York University

Faculty of Science · Department of Chemistry

Joint research on membrane-based ion transport and energy harvesting from salinity gradients. Annual SEED Seminar exchange lecture program. Most recent visit: March 2026.

Membrane Osmotic Energy
🇰🇷 South Korea
Dankook University

College of Engineering · Department of Materials Science

Collaborative research on Li-metal battery interphases and electrolyte additive design. Joint publication program on advanced cathode coating strategies. SEED Seminar: Feb 2026.

Battery Electrolyte
🇰🇷 South Korea
Yonsei University

College of Engineering · School of Chemical Engineering

Partnership on hydrogen evolution reaction catalysis and MXene-based electrode materials. Co-supervised graduate researchers and joint workshop on electrochemical energy conversion.

Hydrogen MXene
🇯🇵 Japan
NIMS / MANA

National Institute for Materials Science · International Center for Materials Nanoarchitectonics

Collaboration on nanostructured catalysts for electrochemical reactions and operando characterization techniques. SEED Seminar lecture: March 2026.

Battery Operando
🇬🇧 United Kingdom
University of Liverpool

Department of Chemistry · Stephenson Institute for Renewable Energy

Joint research on high-throughput computational screening of solid electrolytes and machine learning-guided materials discovery. Co-authorship on AI-accelerated battery materials studies.

Battery Computation
🇺🇸 United States
Washington State University

Voiland School of Chemical Engineering and Bioengineering

Collaborative work on sulfide solid electrolyte processing and all-solid-state battery fabrication. Joint PhD training program in electrochemical energy storage materials.

Battery Sulfide
🇸🇪 Sweden New
Uppsala University

Department of Chemistry-Ångström · Battery Materials Research

Joint collaboration on solid polymer electrolytes in anode-free lithium batteries, co-funded by SSF (Stiftelsen för strategisk forskning) and NSTC (Taiwan) with a combined investment of 70 million SEK. Featured in the 3rd SEED Master Talk, March 2026.

Battery Polymer Electrolyte SSF–NSTC
🇹🇼 Taiwan
National Science and Technology Council (NSTC)

Ministry of Science and Technology · Taiwan

Principal funding body for SEED Center research programs. Key grants include projects on lithium metal battery interface mechanisms, solid electrolyte development, and hydrogen electrocatalysis.
