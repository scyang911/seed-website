About the Center

Our Mission

The Sustainable Electrochemical Energy Development (SEED) Center at National Taiwan University of Science and Technology (NTUST) is dedicated to advancing fundamental and applied research in electrochemical energy technologies.

Established to address the global challenge of sustainable energy, SEED brings together experts across electrochemistry, materials science, chemical engineering, and nanotechnology. Our interdisciplinary approach accelerates the translation of cutting-edge research into practical energy solutions.

The center actively promotes international academic exchanges, industry-academia partnerships, and the training of next-generation researchers in energy-related fields.

Research Axes

🔋
Battery Technology

Our battery team focuses on next-generation energy storage systems that overcome the limitations of conventional lithium-ion technology.

  • Solid-state electrolytes (sulfide, oxide, halide)
  • Lithium and sodium metal batteries
  • Anode-free battery architectures
  • Ni-rich and high-voltage cathode materials
  • Advanced electrolyte design and interphase engineering
  • Operando characterization techniques
🧪
Membrane Engineering

Our membrane team engineers functional membranes for ion transport, energy harvesting, and water purification applications.

  • Nanofluidic and sub-nm ionic membranes
  • MOF/COF hybrid ionic diode membranes
  • Piezoelectric β-PVDF membranes
  • MXene-based desalination membranes
  • Osmotic energy generation
  • Ferroelectric nanopore membranes
⚡
Hydrogen Energy

Our hydrogen energy team develops high-performance electrocatalysts and electrode materials for clean energy conversion.

  • Bimetallic and single-atom alloy catalysts
  • Water electrolysis (HER, OER) catalysts
  • Alkaline hydrogen oxidation reaction (HOR)
  • CO₂ electroreduction and photoreduction
  • Ammonia electrosynthesis
  • Solid oxide electrolysis cells (SOEC)
